# Tim Sullivan

I build practical AI/operator systems, product workflows, and full-stack tools that turn messy real-world processes into something structured, inspectable, and usable.

My strongest work sits at the intersection of:

- product strategy
- AI-assisted workflow design
- source-backed research systems
- mobile/product prototyping
- automation and QA loops
- public-facing packaging that people can actually understand

I am not trying to look like a generic software engineer. My edge is that I can take an unclear problem, find the operational structure inside it, build the workflow, test it, and package it into something a user, client, or team can act on.

## How I work

I care less about showing a pretty demo and more about whether the system survives contact with reality.

That usually means:

- source-backed claims instead of vibes
- explicit constraints and failure modes
- local validation before public claims
- readable proof artifacts
- no unsafe automation without approval
- strong packaging so technical work becomes legible to non-technical people
